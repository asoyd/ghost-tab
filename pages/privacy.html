👻 Ghost Tab

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Short version: Ghost Tab collects nothing. No data ever leaves your device. There are no servers, no analytics, no accounts, and no third-party services of any kind.

What data we collect

None. Ghost Tab does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data.

What is stored locally

Ghost Tab stores the following data on your device only, using chrome.storage.local:

  • Names and URLs of saved Ghost Sessions you create
  • Names and URLs of saved Workspaces you create
  • A flag for whether you have dismissed the incognito setup tip

This data never leaves your browser. It is not encrypted (it contains only URLs and names, not passwords). You can delete it at any time by removing the extension.

Network requests

Ghost Tab makes zero network requests. It has no backend, no API, and no telemetry. You can verify this by inspecting the source code.

Permissions used

  • tabs — to read the current tab's URL when you click "Open current page"
  • contextMenus — to add the right-click "Open in Ghost Tab" menu item
  • storage — to save your sessions and workspaces locally

No permission is used to read page content, track browsing history, or access any personal information.

Third-party services

None. Ghost Tab does not use analytics, crash reporting, advertising, or any external service.

Passwords

Ghost Tab does not store passwords. We recommend using Bitwarden alongside Ghost Tab for credential management.
